## What Nepal Cloud Is

Nepal Cloud is a Kathmandu-based hosting company (headquartered in Thankot) that runs the full spectrum — shared hosting, WordPress hosting, DirectAdmin hosting, VPS, reseller, dedicated servers, and even a free student hosting tier. Infrastructure runs on AMD EPYC processors with NVMe SSD storage and LiteSpeed web servers, with Cloudflare integration layered on top.

Payments accept eSewa, Khalti, ConnectIPS, IME Pay, and bank transfer. For anyone who's ever tried to pay a foreign hosting provider with a Nepali debit card and wanted to throw their laptop out the window, this alone is a genuine selling point.

## The Free Student Hosting — Actually Free

This is the most unusual thing on Nepal Cloud's menu. There's a dedicated free hosting tier for students — no credit card, no trial period expiry, just free.

The FS-1 plan gives you 1 hosted domain, 5 MySQL databases, 10 subdomains, 512 MB NVMe SSD storage, free SSL, DDoS protection, LiteSpeed + LSCache, and Imunify360 security. It's limited (512 MB storage isn't a lot), but for students who need to deploy a first project, build a portfolio site, or learn how cPanel works, it removes the cost barrier entirely.

## Who Is Nepal Cloud Actually For?

**Students and beginners**: The free FS-1 hosting tier is genuinely useful here. You get a real hosting environment with SSL, LiteSpeed, and Imunify360 — not some stripped-down sandbox. Deploy your first project, learn how DNS works, and figure out cPanel without spending anything.

**Freelancers and agencies building WordPress sites**: The WP Business plan at Rs.150/month gives unlimited domains and unlimited storage, which means you can host every client site on one account. The LiteSpeed + LSCache combo handles typical WordPress workloads well.

**Developers running applications**: VPS 1 at Rs.1,980/month with 4 vCPU, 6 GB RAM, and 100 GB NVMe is a solid entry-level production environment. Full root access, Ubuntu, SSH — the basics are there. Multiple server locations give you flexibility depending on where your users are.

**Anyone annoyed by international payment friction**: This is legitimately one of the main reasons to choose a local provider. No need for an international credit card or a complicated workaround.
